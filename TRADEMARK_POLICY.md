# Trademark Policy

This Trademark Policy explains how names, logos, and other brand indicators related to FacetDeck may be used.

## 1) Scope

The following are considered trademarks or brand assets of the FacetDeck project maintainers (the "Marks"):

- "FacetDeck" name
- FacetDeck logos, icons, and visual brand identifiers
- Official product/service naming used by maintainers

## 2) Open Source Code vs Trademark Rights

- Source code in this repository is licensed under `AGPL-3.0-or-later`.
- The software license does **not** grant rights to use the Marks.
- Trademark rights are governed separately by this policy.

## 3) Allowed Uses (Without Prior Written Permission)

You may use the Marks only for factual, non-confusing references, such as:

- Saying your software is "compatible with FacetDeck"
- Linking to this repository and documentation
- Describing unmodified distributions of this project

Conditions:

- Use must be truthful and not misleading.
- Use must not imply sponsorship, endorsement, partnership, certification, or official status.
- Use must not make your product/service appear to be the official FacetDeck offering.

## 4) Prohibited Uses

Without prior written permission from maintainers, you may **not**:

- Use "FacetDeck" (or confusingly similar names) in your product or company name
- Use FacetDeck logos/icons in your product, website, app, social profile, or marketing materials
- Present a fork, hosted deployment, plugin market, template market, or SaaS as an official FacetDeck service
- Use domains/subdomains/account names that are likely to confuse users about official origin
- Suggest endorsement or affiliation when none exists

Examples of prohibited claims include:

- "Official FacetDeck Cloud" (when not operated by maintainers)
- "FacetDeck Managed Service" (when not operated by maintainers)
- "FacetDeck Community Marketplace" (when not officially operated)

## 5) Forks and Modified Hosted Services

If you fork or modify this project:

- You must use a clearly different product and service name.
- You should remove or replace FacetDeck logos and primary brand identifiers.
- You must not describe your deployment in a way that confuses users into believing it is official.

## 6) Nominative Reference Guidance

Reasonable referential use is generally acceptable, for example:

- "Built on FacetDeck open-source code"
- "Plugin compatible with FacetDeck"

Best practices:

- Keep "FacetDeck" less prominent than your own brand.
- Include a disclaimer such as: "Not affiliated with or endorsed by the FacetDeck maintainers."

## 7) Reporting Misuse

To report suspected trademark misuse, open a private security/governance contact as described in `SECURITY.md` or maintainer contact channels.

Please include:

- Link(s) and screenshot(s)
- A brief description of the confusing use
- Relevant dates and context

## 8) Reservation of Rights

All trademark rights are reserved by the maintainers except as explicitly stated in this policy.

Maintainers may update this policy when necessary to protect users from confusion and impersonation.
